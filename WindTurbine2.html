Wind Turbine Noise - Predicted noise in the far field

The prediction of the acoustic pressure from trailing-edge noise, requires a model for the scattering of the pressure fluctuations at the edge. In this simple example, we have a look at Amiet's model, which transforms the spectrum of the pressure fluctuations at the trailing edge, into the spectrum of the acoustic pressure in the far-field. The power spectrum density of what it is heard at a specific receiver position, as discussed in the lectures, is given by the expression below, and all parameters are explained by clicking on the nomenclature button besides the formula. For simplicity, we are first assuming a wing with a NACA0012 profile, having a defined aspect ratio. After that, with the buttons on the top-right of the page, you can see what happens by changing some of the parameters. All the graphs are obtained according to the formulas from:

Yuan Tian, Benjamin Cotté, Antoine Chaigne. Wind Turbine Noise Modelling Based on Amiet’s Theory. 5th International Meeting on Wind Turbine Noise, Aug 2013, Denver, CO, United States.
